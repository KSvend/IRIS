# Desk Review Update: Hate Speech and Violent Extremism in East Africa
## UNDP MERLx IRIS Programme — October 2025 to March 2026
### Countries: Kenya, Somalia, South Sudan

---

## Methodology Note

This desk review update covers the period from October 2025 through March 2026, supplementing an existing UNDP-commissioned assessment on violent extremist narratives and hate speech in Kenya, Somalia, and South Sudan. Over 100 individual search queries were executed across primary institutional sources (UN agencies, national parliaments, regional bodies), international media, academic databases, fact-checking organisations, and civil society publications. Six parallel research streams were conducted: country-specific investigations for Kenya, Somalia, and South Sudan; cross-cutting thematic analysis; institutional and policy mapping; and academic and grey literature review.

All findings include date, source, URL, and key takeaway. Items marked **[FLAG]** or **[NEW/SIGNIFICANT UPDATE]** represent developments that materially change or substantially update earlier assessments. Primary and institutional sources were prioritised; secondary and news sources are clearly attributed. All URLs were verified as of 20 March 2026.

---

## Executive Summary

The October 2025 – March 2026 period has been marked by escalating conflict, deepening political fragmentation, and a rapidly evolving digital threat environment across all three countries.

**Kenya** experienced its most significant political disruption in a generation with the death of Raila Odinga on 15 October 2025, fundamentally reshaping the opposition landscape ahead of the 2027 elections. The National Cohesion and Integration Commission (NCIC) launched unprecedented institutional tools — including formal Social Media Monitoring Guidelines and a National Action Plan on Hate Speech — while simultaneously documenting that online hate speech is directly fueling radicalisation. A landmark Court of Appeal ruling struck down the "fake news" provisions of the Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act, and the Senate began debating Africa's most comprehensive AI governance bill. Al-Shabaab activity in northeastern Kenya intensified, with 61 terror incidents documented in a single year and a foiled Nairobi attack plot during Ramadan 2026. The application of terrorism charges to the Shakahola cult case set a precedent for how Kenya's counter-terrorism framework applies to non-jihadist extremism.

**Somalia** faces a convergence of security, political, and diplomatic crises. Al-Shabaab's 2025 Shabelle Offensive reversed years of government territorial gains, and the Africa Center for Strategic Studies warned Somalia risks becoming a "jihadist state." The ATMIS-to-AUSSOM peacekeeping transition faces a critical funding shortfall of approximately $190 million. Political fragmentation deepened dramatically: a unilateral permanent constitution was adopted in March 2026 (opposition boycotted), three federal member states severed ties with Mogadishu, and election talks collapsed in February 2026. Israel's recognition of Somaliland on 26 December 2025 triggered cascading geopolitical consequences — Somalia cancelled all agreements with the UAE, and inflammatory rhetoric from the Defence Minister escalated tensions. The first documented AI deepfake inciting inter-clan violence in Somalia's electoral context was recorded in December 2025.

**South Sudan** is experiencing its most severe humanitarian and security deterioration since the 2018 peace agreement. The trial of First Vice President Riek Machar on treason charges reached its 60th session by March 2026, with a Small Arms Survey report directly contradicting the prosecution's central claims. Violence spread from Upper Nile to Jonglei, displacing over 268,000 people, and the Ruweng massacre of March 2026 killed 169–178 people. General Johnson Olony's "spare no one" speech in January 2026 — explicitly targeting the Nuer community — drew emergency condemnation from the UN Commission on Human Rights. Elections scheduled for 22 December 2026 have received only 4% of required funding, and voter registration has not begun. The R-ARCSS peace agreement is, in the words of RJMEC, "in serious jeopardy."

**Cross-cutting trends** include: a projected 400–600% increase in AI disinformation campaigns across Africa by Q4 2026; persistent detection gaps for East African languages in deepfake detection tools; platform governance retreat by Meta, X, and TikTok creating measurable increases in hate speech circulation; and growing evidence that diaspora communities serve as both conflict accelerators and potential peace enablers through digital platforms.

#### 1.1 NPS–NCIC Joint Investigations: 12 Hate Speech Cases Under Probe
- **Date:** 25 February 2026
- **Source:** Capital FM Kenya / Allafrica (reporting on Senate testimony by Inspector General Douglas Kanja)
- **URL:** [Capital FM Kenya](https://www.capitalfm.co.ke/news/2026/02/kenya-police-probe-hate-speech-cases-2027-elections/)
- **Relevance:** Hate speech enforcement pipeline
- **Key takeaway:** Inspector General Douglas Kanja told the Senate Standing Committee on National Cohesion that the NPS and NCIC investigated 12 cases of hate speech, incitement to violence, cyber harassment, and dissemination of inflammatory content over the preceding year. #### 3.1 Amnesty International Report: State Use of Digital Tools Against Protesters
- **Date:** 18 November 2025
- **Source:** Amnesty International
- **URL:** [Amnesty International](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2025/11/kenyan-weaponized-social-media-and-digital-tools-to-suppress-gen-z-protests/)
- **Relevance:** State-sponsored digital repression
- **Key takeaway:** Amnesty International's landmark report documented systematic, coordinated state deployment of digital platforms to suppress Gen Z protests (June 2024–July 2025). # Appendix: Research Gaps and Limitations

The following research gaps were documented across all six research streams. They represent areas where available open-source materials were insufficient to reach definitive conclusions.

---

## Kenya

1. **Kenyan diaspora-specific hate speech production**: While evidence exists of diaspora political activity (e.g., Gachagua-associated deepfakes), dedicated research on diaspora-driven hate speech targeting Kenya was not found.
2. **NCIC Amendment Bill parliamentary status**: As of March 2026, the bill was awaiting parliamentary action; its passage timeline is unclear.
3. **Religious Organisations Bill outcome**: The bill faces unified religious opposition; passage is uncertain.
4. **Al-Shabaab digital recruitment details**: The State of National Security Report identifies encrypted platforms but specific operational detail remains classified.

## Somalia

1. **Burao protests 2025**: Multiple references to "2025 protests in Burao" but specific events, dates, triggers, and narrative content not fully documented. Further primary source research recommended.
2. **TUBSAN capacity assessment**: TUBSAN's website does not publish specific activity dates. Capacity data (~30 employees) remains from 2024 ISS article.
3. **Al-Shabaab amnesty uptake**: No public data on actual defection numbers following February–March 2026 amnesty offers.
4. **Clubhouse and audio-based diaspora activity**: No specific documentation found for Somali political narrative activity in the review period.
5. **"Langaab" usage documentation**: EUAA references general minority discrimination but does not specifically document "Langaab" dehumanising language. Requires Somali-language social media monitoring.
6. **IS-Somalia amnesty response**: No data on response to the March 2026 IS-inclusive amnesty.

## South Sudan

1. **"Shelohim Majan" / AI-generated images**: No primary-source documentation found. Recommend direct outreach to DRF/211Check.
2. **Clubhouse-specific mobilisation**: Not indexed in open sources. Recommend targeted community research.
3. **South Sudan News Agency (SSNA) bill**: Status unclear; requires verification with National Legislative Assembly.
4. **MTN free Facebook continuation**: No 2026-specific confirmation found. Direct contact with MTN South Sudan recommended.
5. **Lomuro "hostile/friendly" consequences**: No formal legal challenge filed; classification remains on record.
6. **Hate speech prosecutions under Cybercrime Act 2026**: Act is new; monitor for first prosecutions and selective application patterns.

## Cross-Cutting

1. **Platform language capacity for Dinka, Nuer, Juba Arabic**: No public transparency data from Meta, TikTok, or X on moderator capacity for South Sudan's key conflict languages.
2. **Christchurch Call East Africa engagement**: No East Africa-specific activities identified. Gap in direct regional outreach.
3. **GIFCT East Africa capacity building**: No specific activities during the review period.
4. **Journey to Extremism follow-up**: No new edition or update identified. The 2023 second edition remains the most recent.
5. **USIP capacity**: Severely disrupted by US administration; no East Africa outputs during the period.
